Tuberculosis(TB)

About Tuberculosis(TB)

Tuberculosis, or TB, is a bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs. It has been around for thousands of years, and there is evidence of TB infection in mummies from ancient Egypt. TB was a major cause of death in the 19th and early 20th centuries, but it has become less common in developed countries due to improved living conditions and medical treatments. However, TB is still a significant problem in many parts of the world.Tuberculosis (TB) is an infectious disease caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis. It primarily affects the lungs but can also affect other parts of the body. It spreads through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. Symptoms include coughing, chest pain, fever, and fatigue. TB can be treated with a combination of antibiotics.

Signs of Tuberculosis(TB)

Some common signs and symptoms of TB include: 

  • Coughing that lasts for more than three weeks 
  • Chest pain
  • Coughing up blood
  • Fatigue
  • Unintended weight loss
  • Loss of appetite
  • Night sweats
  • Chills
  • Fever
  • Shortness of breath
  • Swollen glands.
Remember that these symptoms can also be caused by other illnesses, so it's important not to jump to conclusions.
