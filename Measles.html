About Measles

In the 9th century, a Persian doctor published one of the earliest written accounts regarding measles. In 1757, Francis Home, a Scottish physician, established that the infectious agent in the blood of those affected was responsible for the disease. By 1912, reporting all diagnosed cases was mandatory in the United States; each year around 6,000 related deaths were reported. Prior to 91 1963 when vaccination became available, nearly all children had contracted measles by age 15. It's estimated that 3 to 4 million were infected on an annual basis; during this pe, 400-500 people perished, 48,000 were hospitalized and 1,000 experienced encephalitis from measles complications.

  • Koplik spots Tiny white spots (Koplik spots) may appear inside the mouth two to three days after symptoms begin.
The skin of a patient after 3 days of measles infection:
  • Three to five days after symptoms begin, a rash breaks out.
  • It usually begins as flat red spots that appear on the face at the hairline and spread downward to the neck, trunk, arms, legs, and feet.
  • Small raised bumps may also appear on top of the flat red spots.
  • The spots may become joined together as they spread from the head to the rest of the body.
  • When the rash appears, a person’s fever may spike to more than 104° Fahrenheit

how to protect

Getting vaccinated is the best way to protect yourself and others against
measles. The measles vaccine is a routine vaccination given to children
in the United States.vaccine is usually given in two doses. It’s recommended
that children get the first dose when they are 12 to 15 months old and the
second dose when they are 4 to 6 years old. iodThe 1 measles vaccine is a combination vaccine that also protects against mumps and rubella.
