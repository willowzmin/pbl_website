About Influenza

The 1918 influenza pandemic was the most severe in recent history. It was caused by an H1N1 virus with genes of avian origin. Although there is no universal consensus regarding where the virus originated, it spread worldwide during 1918-1919. Influenza

Sign Influenza (flu) can cause mild to severe illness, and at times can lead to death. Flu symptoms usually come on suddenly. People who have flu often feel some or all of these symptoms:

  • fever* or feeling feverish/chills
  • cough
  • sore throat
  • runny or stuffy nose
  • muscle or body aches
  • headaches
  • fatigue (tiredness)
  • some people may have vomiting and diarrhea, though this is more common in children than adults.

  1. Stay home when you are sick.

    If possible, stay home from work, school, and errands when you are sick. This will help prevent spreading your illness to others.

  2. Clean your hands.

    Washing your hands often will help protect you from germs. If soap and water are not available, use an alcohol-based hand rub.

  3. Avoid touching your eyes, nose, or mouth.

    Germs can be spread when a person touches something that is contaminated with germs and then touches his or her eyes, nose, or mouth. Practice other good health habits
